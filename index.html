Picture the Build

Imagine building your dream home here.

Picture your first morning on the ridge. Coffee on a deck that doesn't exist yet, looking out over cedar canopy toward a tree line still holding fog. No neighbor's roofline in that view. No fence. Just the forest that borders your property, going nowhere.

Design the great room to catch the afternoon light coming off the ridge, or angle a second-story window toward Mount Baker on the horizon. Frame the kitchen around the cedars you choose to keep. Build a patio around the glacial boulder that's been sitting on this lot since long before Glenhaven had a name, and let it do the landscaping work you'd otherwise hire someone to fake.

In the evening, walk the trail behind the lots down to Cain Lake, or stay outside long after dark under a sky the streetlights haven't reached yet. This is the version of home that starts with land good enough to build the rest of your life around.

The Region

Life on this ridge moves at the pace of the seasons, not the commute. Mornings might start with a paddle across Lake Whatcom or a quiet walk down to Cain Lake before the rest of the county is awake. On clear days, Mount Baker holds the horizon like a permanent fixture. When you want a town, Bellingham is twenty minutes down the hill, and Fairhaven's brick storefronts and harbor are barely further than that.

The Area

The highest-value ground nearby.

Most listings say "close to trails." Here's the actual inventory — the destinations that matter most, and how long it takes to reach them.

Property photography and drone video throughout this page are owner-filmed. Area and lifestyle photography represent the destinations described.

Cain Lake

Walking distance

Glenhaven's own lake — swimming, kayaks, trout, and the community dock. The valley the property overlooks runs down toward its shore.

Lake Whatcom

≈ 6 min to boat launch

Ten miles of freshwater stretching from Bellingham into the hills — boating, fishing, and shoreline parks. Glenhaven sits just over the ridge from its south end.

Lake Samish

≈ 10 min

A quieter third lake in the same corner of the county — a shoreline community, fishing, and another reminder of how much water surrounds this ridge.

Sudden Valley

≈ 15 min

The master-planned community on Lake Whatcom — golf course, marina, beaches — where homes regularly sell from $700K into the millions. Proof this corner of the county carries serious value.

Mount Baker

On the horizon · ski ≈ 90 min

A 10,781-foot glaciated volcano — and the view potential from a future second story on these lots. Its ski area holds the world record for snowfall in a single season.

Galbraith Mountain

≈ 20 min

65+ miles of purpose-built mountain bike trail on the ridge between Lake Whatcom and Bellingham — one of the best-known riding networks in the country.

Chuckanut Drive & the Chuckanuts

≈ 25–30 min

Washington's famous coast road, carved along the cliffs where the Cascades' foothills meet saltwater — oyster bars, viewpoints, and Oyster Dome's summit view of the bay and islands.

Fairhaven

≈ 25 min

Bellingham's historic brick district on the bay — bookstores, restaurants, the village green, and the Alaska ferry terminal. The kind of neighborhood other towns try to build from scratch.

Anacortes & the ferries

≈ 40 min

The gateway to the islands — a working marina town where Washington State Ferries depart for the San Juans all day. Boat owners: this is where yours lives.

The San Juan Islands

Ferry from Anacortes

172 named islands in the rain shadow — orcas, harbors, farm stands, and some of the most protected sailing water in North America. A day trip from this ridge.

Bellingham

≈ 20 min

The city that anchors it all — about 100,000 people, Western Washington University, hospitals, and a food-and-outdoors culture that keeps landing on "best town" lists.

I-5 & BLI Airport

≈ 8 min · ≈ 30 min

The detail that changes everything: this hilltop feels remote, but the freeway on-ramp is eight minutes away — and Bellingham International is thirty.

V

Vancouver, BC & YVR

≈ 90 min · border ≈ 45 min

One of the world's most livable cities, close enough for dinner — the Peace Arch border crossing is under an hour away. YVR opens direct routes across Asia and Europe.

S

Seattle & SEA

≈ 90 min

Ninety minutes south: the region's economic engine, pro sports, and Sea-Tac's global network. Close enough for a meeting or a flight, far enough that none of it follows you home.

Why This Property

Why these two lots — not just any Glenhaven lot.

Glenhaven has other homesites from time to time, but none combine these things in a single purchase. Some of this is specific to Lots 15 & 16 versus their neighbors on the same ridge; some is true of Glenhaven as a whole and worth knowing before comparing this ridge to land anywhere else in the county.

The Only Homesite at the Top of the Ridge

Other Glenhaven parcels sit at varying points on the hillside. Lots 15 & 16 are widely regarded as the highest homesite in the community — an elevation advantage for a future build that isn't available to buy anywhere else in the neighborhood.

Forest on One Side, Not a Fence Line

Most platted lots back up to another lot or a road. These back directly onto forest that is not part of any building lot — a rear boundary that stays a tree line instead of eventually becoming a neighbor's siding.

Two Lots, Two Kinds of Ready

A typical single-lot purchase here is one condition: cleared or wooded, and nothing else. This purchase is both — Lot 16 already cleared and carrying an existing septic design, Lot 15 left natural with mature standing cedars. Build now, keep a private greenbelt next door, or both.

A Lodge and Maintained Roads, Without the Isolation

Some rural land purchases mean building a sense of community from nothing. Glenhaven already has its own clubhouse and maintained roads in place — real infrastructure around a homesite that still feels like the edge of the wild.

Between Two Lakes, Not Just Near One

Cain Lake is walking distance from these lots; the Lake Whatcom boat launch is about six minutes by car. Few homesites anywhere put two separate lakes this close together.

Remote Feeling, City-Close Reality

I-5 is about eight minutes away, and Bellingham — roughly 100,000 people, a university, hospitals — is about twenty. Land with comparable elevation and privacy elsewhere in the county usually trades that quiet for a much longer drive. Here it doesn't.
